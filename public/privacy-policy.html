Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 29, 2026  ·  Effective immediately

EdGuard is a biometric identity verification platform for academic institutions. We take privacy seriously. Biometric data is processed locally on-device wherever possible and never sold to third parties.

1. Data We Collect

When you use EdGuard, we may collect the following data to perform identity verification:

  • Identity data: First name, last name, student ID, institution, course
  • Biometric data: Facial image (liveness capture), voice sample, cognitive reaction-time signals
  • Device data: Device model, OS version, session timestamps
  • Behavioral telemetry: Tap patterns, interaction timing (for anomaly detection only)

2. How We Use Your Data

Data collected through EdGuard is used exclusively for:

  • Verifying student identity before exam sessions
  • Authenticating identity before grant or scholarship disbursement
  • Building a biometric baseline during enrollment
  • Generating post-quantum cryptographic signatures of verification events

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, or any purpose unrelated to identity verification.

3. Data Storage & Security

Biometric profiles are stored in an encrypted database (AES-256-GCM) with post-quantum signing (NIST FIPS 203/204). Data at rest is secured using industry-standard encryption. Data in transit is protected by TLS 1.3.

Biometric data is stored only for the duration required by the institution's verification programme. Users may request deletion at any time.

4. Data Sharing

EdGuard does not sell, rent, or trade your personal or biometric data. Data may be shared only with:

  • The institution that deployed EdGuard for your verification session
  • Infrastructure providers (cloud hosting) under strict data processing agreements
  • Legal authorities if required by applicable law

5. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access the data we hold about you
  • Request correction or deletion of your biometric profile
  • Withdraw consent at any time (subject to your institution's requirements)
  • Lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority

6. Children's Privacy

EdGuard is not intended for use by individuals under 16 years of age without explicit institutional and parental consent.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy. The latest version will always be available at this URL. Continued use of EdGuard after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
